Features

Learn about our features.- Discovery of Radioactive Elements: - Polonium: Named after her homeland, Poland, this element emits alpha and beta particles. - Radium: Emits alpha particles and was used in radiation therapy. - Radioactivity Research: - Coined the term "radioactivity" to describe the emission of rays by uranium compounds. - Demonstrated that radioactivity is an atomic property, not a molecular interaction. - Portable X-ray Machine: - Invented during World War I to diagnose injuries near the battlefront. - Nicknamed "Little Curies," these units saved countless lives. - Radiation Therapy: - Pioneered the use of high doses of radiation to treat cancer.

About

Get to know us better.## About Marie Curie: ## Early Life - Born to Science teachers Bronislawa and Wladyslaw - Mother died when Marie was ten - Completed high school at 15, obtaining a medal for best student - Interested in science from an early age ## Career - Left Russia in 1891 to study at the University of Paris - Discovered radioactive elements polonium and radium - First woman to win a Nobel Prize and first person to win two Nobel Prizes in different fields (physics and chemistry) - Founded the Curie Institutes in Paris and Warsaw for medical research. Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who made groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the first person to win two Nobel Prizes in different fields (physics and chemistry). Her work paved the way for advancements in medicine, energy, and scientific research.
